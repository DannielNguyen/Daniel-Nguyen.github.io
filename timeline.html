The Evolution of AI

Early Foundations
1956
Dartmouth Workshop
Widely recognized as the birth of AI as a formal discipline. Researchers proposed that "every aspect of learning or intelligence" could be simulated.
1966
ELIZA
Joseph Weizenbaum's chatbot demonstrated early natural language processing — a precursor to conversational AI.
AI Winters
1974
First AI Winter Begins
  • Funding cuts and unmet expectations slowed progress.
  • Symbolic AI hit computational limits.
1980
Expert Systems Boom
Businesses adopted rule-based systems like XCON. AI saw its first major commercial wave.
1987
Second AI Winter
  • Collapse of the expert systems market.
  • Specialized hardware (Lisp machines) became obsolete.
Modern Era
1997
IBM Deep Blue
First machine to defeat a reigning world chess champion. A landmark in symbolic search and brute-force computation.
2006
Deep Learning Revival
  • Geoffrey Hinton and colleagues reintroduced neural networks with improved training.
  • Sparked modern machine learning momentum.
2020s
Foundation Models & Cloud AI
  • Large language models (LLMs) reshape automation, analytics, and creativity.
  • Cloud platforms (Azure, AWS, Google Cloud) enable global-scale AI deployment.
